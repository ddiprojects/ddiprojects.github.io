We are dedicated to reaching and empowering needy girls across Laikipia County

  • Menstrual Health & Hygiene Management

    Menstrual hygiene management (MHM) is an essential aspect of hygiene for women and adolescent girls between menarche and menopause. Despite being an important issue concerning women and girls in the menstruating age group MHM is often overlooked. Women and girls in low income/rural settings have low awareness of hygienic practices and lack culturally appropriate materials for menstrual hygiene management (MHM) practices.Menstruation and associated activities are surrounded by silence, shame and social taboos. DDI endeavours to provide this information and sanitary products to needy girls in Laikipia through partnerships with learning institutions, community groups and local administrators

  • Conservation Education

    Fostering environmental and wildlife conservation awareness through art and instilling eco-consciousness to the school going kids. One of the tools we use is a kids poetry and colouring book that I co-wrote in 2014 "Let`s Go Wild." Learning by doing is a core pillar that we use, training schools and community groups to set up Waste Management Projects.

About DDI and the #PeriodsDontPauseForPandemics Campaign

After schools shut down due to the COVID-19, we began the #PeriodsDon'tPauseForPandemics campaign. We reached out to needy girls who do not have access to sanitary towels and to equip them with menstrual health knowledge. Our grassroots campaign team comprises of our members, local youths and chiefs.
